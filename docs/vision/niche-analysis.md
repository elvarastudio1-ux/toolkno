# Niche Selection Analysis

## Decision Summary

**Selected niche (long-term):** Creator Economy Tools
**Entry niche (months 1–6):** Writing & Content Software
**Second-stage expansion (months 6–18):** Creator Economy Tools
**Third stage (Year 2+):** SEO Software

This decision is documented as ADR-001 in `docs/decisions/ADR-001-niche-selection.md`.

---

## Decision Methodology

Evaluated 14 software niches against a weighted decision matrix with 7 dimensions. The dimensions and their weights reflect ToolKno's primary business objective: build the most trusted software decision platform while maximizing long-term affiliate revenue.

### Weighting Rationale

| Dimension | Weight | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Affiliate revenue potential | 25% | Primary revenue mechanism — must be viable |
| Trust barrier to entry | 20% | Trust is the core moat — high trust barriers protect the position |
| Moat potential | 15% | Can ToolKno become genuinely hard to dislodge in this niche? |
| SEO opportunity | 15% | Organic traffic funds the flywheel without paid acquisition |
| Newsletter audience fit | 10% | The newsletter is the long-term asset — does this niche subscribe? |
| Ease of entry | 10% | One founder; difficulty must be proportionate to capacity |
| Speed to authority | 5% | How quickly can ToolKno earn credibility in this niche? |

## Top 3 Niche Analysis

### #1: Creator Economy Tools (8.40)

**Why it wins:**

The creator economy is the fastest-growing software buyer category in 2024–2026. Creators and independent publishers are paying for more software subscriptions than any time in history, have high switching intent (they try many tools), and actively seek independent recommendations because vendor marketing is pervasive.

**Revenue ceiling is exceptional:** Beehiiv: 50% revenue share for the subscriber's first year (plans start at $42/month). Kajabi: 30% lifetime recurring commission (plans start at $149/month). Podia, Teachable, ConvertKit, Gumroad, Ghost — all have affiliate programs paying 20–30%+ with high plan values. A single reader who purchases Kajabi through a ToolKno link earns ~$45/month for the life of their subscription.

**Newsletter fit is perfect:** A newsletter about creator tools is read by the exact audience most likely to upgrade creator tools. The newsletter subscriber is simultaneously the target customer and the editorial authority's primary distribution.

**Moat is defensible:** No clear, editorially rigorous, independent creator tool review publication exists at scale. The existing players (The Tilt, Creatorly Media) are creator-focused but not software-focused. The space exists; it is not yet occupied by a trusted reference.

**Why not immediately:** The audience requires the reviewer to have genuine creator credibility. Jumping directly to creator tool reviews without an established reader base risks low authority. The entry strategy is Writing & Content Software first — which overlaps substantially with creator tools and establishes the editorial credibility.

### #2: Writing & Content Software (8.10)

**Why it's second:**

Writing & Content Software has the highest SEO opportunity (established search demand, high-intent queries like "best grammar checker" and "Grammarly alternatives"), the fastest path to authority (the free tool audience is already writing-focused), and the lowest difficulty to enter (tools are SaaS, no enterprise complexity, features are evaluable in weeks).

**Why it's the entry niche, not the long-term niche:** The affiliate economics are weaker than Creator Economy. Grammarly pays 20% on $12–$30/month subscriptions. ProWritingAid pays 40% but on lower plan values. The ceiling is lower. However, these are the right early affiliate relationships because they convert from ToolKno's existing free tool audience.

**Strategic relationship to Creator Economy:** Writing tools (Grammarly, Hemingway, ProWritingAid) are used by creators. Reviews in the writing niche build credibility that transfers directly to creator audience trust when the expansion begins.

### #3: SEO Software (7.50)

**Why it's third:**

The SEO software market has the highest affiliate revenue potential of any niche (Semrush: $200 per subscription, Ahrefs partner programs, Surfer SEO 25% recurring) and strong search demand. The reason it ranks third rather than higher: difficulty. SEO tool evaluations require expertise that takes time to establish credibility in, the comparison is technically complex, and the audience is highly skeptical of reviews that don't demonstrate deep technical SEO knowledge. This is the right niche for Year 2, when ToolKno has established editorial authority.

## Eliminated Niches — Key Reasons

**HR & Payroll / Accounting:** High affiliate revenue but extremely low newsletter fit (a creator audience does not subscribe to HR tool newsletters), very long sales cycles, and high compliance complexity in the content.

**Social Media Management:** Low affiliate economics, highly commoditized review space (every marketing blog covers it), weak newsletter fit (social media managers read social media, not software newsletters).

**Design Tools:** Most top design tools (Figma, Adobe Creative Cloud) have no meaningful affiliate programs or pay nominal commissions. Revenue ceiling eliminates this niche from consideration despite reasonable trust and SEO scores.
